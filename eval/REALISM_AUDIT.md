# Realism audit — why the outputs still feel synthetic

Date: 2026-06-11 · Audited material: full-catalog sweep `20260611-070715`
(+ hoop re-run `20260611-071658`), inspected at zoom against the inputs and
catalog product photos. Evidence crops live in [`eval/audit/`](audit/)
(generated by [`audit/make_crops.py`](audit/make_crops.py)); region metrics
quoted below come from the same script.

Classification codes: **A** prompt · **B** catalog asset · **C** input photo
· **D** evaluation · **E** model · **F** architecture (API path / pipeline).

---

## The headline root cause

**The API path re-synthesizes every pixel of the photo on every edit; it never
composites.** Measured on "untouched" regions (same box, input → output edge
stddev):

| region (should be unchanged) | input | output | change |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| wall behind gem-set necklace | 26.6 | 21.6 | −19% micro-texture |
| cheek, gem-set necklace case | 31.9 | 26.5 | −17% |
| face, oxford case | 38.6 | 33.0 | −15% |
| wall, wrap-dress case | 23.1 | 20.4 | −12% |

Every output is a slightly smoothed re-rendering of the whole scene: faces are
re-drawn (the three clothing outputs read as "sisters, not the same woman" at
zoom — eyebrows, lip tone and every hair wisp differ), sneakers get new lace
patterns, grain is everywhere reduced. No amount of prompt engineering can fix
this, because the model *cannot return original pixels* — it can only
re-generate them. Classification: **F/E, confidence: very high, impact: high**
(this is the floor under everything else).

## The strategic questions

**Is the current model fundamentally capable of the target realism?**
Partially. `gemini-3.1-flash-image` is excellent at geometry, placement,
identity-at-a-glance and reference-following, but it consistently fails three
things prompts cannot reach: (1) returning original pixels (it re-samples
everything), (2) depth-ordered occlusion against hair, (3) physically
consistent micro-illumination (contact shadows, falloff, color bleed) on
inserted objects. (1) is architectural; (2) and (3) may improve one tier up —
the Pro-tier Nano Banana model (`gemini-3-pro-image`) is a one-line,
few-generations A/B experiment this repo is already configured for
(`NANOBANANA_MODEL`).

**Is more prompt engineering likely to produce meaningful gains?** No — this
is the key conclusion. The v2 prompts already encode the correct physics
(contact shadows, occlusion, grain, scale anchors) and the model demonstrably
*partially* complies with each. The remaining gap is between "described" and
"simulated"; iterating the description has hit diminishing returns. Prompts
are no longer the bottleneck; keep them frozen except for per-finding
one-line hints (N2) and the FM-E user-choice constraint (C4).

**Preprocessing needed?** Mildly: spec-neutralize/white-balance the studio
product shots (N3), add physical dimensions to catalog metadata (N5), and fix
the two benchmark inputs that violate the app's own guidance (E1, B2).

**Post-processing needed?** Yes — highest ROI in the whole audit. A
pixel-preserving compositor (diff mask → keep original pixels outside the
edit, feathered seam) eliminates the headline cause for every category at
once, and grain+color harmonization of the edit region addresses N3/N4/E3/C3.
All Pillow/numpy, no new APIs, no quota.

**Segmentation / pose / region workflows?** Segmentation yes, in two places:
hair mattes for earring re-occlusion (E1) and person/garment masks to make the
compositor and the D1 metrics robust. Pose estimation only matters if moving
to warping-based VTON.

**Would local inference unlock anything?** For clothing, yes: open VTON models
(IDM-VTON, CatVTON, OOTDiffusion class) do explicit garment warping with
mask-exact preservation of everything else — that solves C1/C2/C5 by
construction, at the cost of GPU infrastructure and integration effort. For
jewelry, local masked inpainting (FLUX.1-Fill / SDXL-inpaint + IP-Adapter)
gives pixel-exact preservation but risks losing the ornate-product fidelity
that Nano Banana is genuinely good at. The pragmatic path is hybrid: keep Nano
Banana as the renderer, wrap it in the local compositor/harmonization layer.

## Ranked roadmap

Ranked by expected realism gain × probability of success ÷ effort.

| # | Improvement | Fixes | Realism gain | Complexity | Effort | P(success) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Pixel-preserving compositor** — diff-mask the edit region, paste back original pixels everywhere else with a feathered seam; face-lock for clothing | headline cause, C1, C5, R3, N4 partially | **High** | Low | ~1-2 days | High (border drift is already ≤2.4/255, so seams are benign) |
| 2 | **Model-tier A/B** — `NANOBANANA_MODEL=gemini-3-pro-image`, re-run 4 hard cases, compare rubric | N1, N2, C2, B4, E3 | Potentially high | Trivial | hours + ~8 generations | Medium-high |
| 3 | **Edit-region grain + color harmonization** (post, after #1) — measure input noise spectrum and scene color stats, apply to the composited edit region | N3, N4, E3, C3, R1, B3 | Medium-high | Low-medium | ~2-3 days | High |
| 4 | **Benchmark input fixes** — visible-ear face photo for earring cases; natural raised-hand pose for bracelets; keep old inputs as extra hard cases | E1, E2, B2 (validity) | Medium (truthful measurement; better model guidance) | Low | ~half day + ~6 generations | High |
| 5 | **Region-aware metrics** — parity on the unchanged region only; "object cleaner than scene" detector | D1, D2, breton false-positive | Medium (detection) | Low-medium | ~1-2 days | High |
| 6 | **Hair re-occlusion module** — hair matte (rembg/SAM-class) from the input, re-composite strands over jewelry | E1 residual, N-chain edges under hair | High for earrings | Medium | ~3-5 days | Medium |
| 7 | **FM-E user choice** — UI toggle ("keep my leggings/socks") injected as one prompt line | C4 | Low-medium | Low | ~half day | High |
| 8 | **Catalog asset upgrade** — drape-revealing or on-model garment shots; spec-neutralized jewelry shots; physical dimensions in metadata | C2, N3, N5 | Medium | Low-medium | sourcing-bound | High |
| 9 | **Second-pass shadow/relight edit** — feed the output back: "add the contact shadows this jewelry would cast" (only with #1 in place to contain regeneration) | N1, B1 | Medium | Low | doubles per-item quota | Medium-low |
| 10 | **Local VTON for clothing** (IDM-VTON / CatVTON class) | C1, C2, C5 by construction | High (clothing only) | High (GPU, serving) | weeks | Medium-high |
| 11 | **Local masked inpainting for jewelry** (FLUX-Fill + IP-Adapter) | preservation by construction | Medium (fidelity risk on ornate pieces) | High | weeks | Medium |

Items 1-5 are the recommended next sprint: no architecture change, almost no
quota, and they attack the two highest-impact causes (pixel re-synthesis and
the composited-object look) plus the measurement blind spot. Items 10-11 are
the long-term path to "truly photorealistic" if the hybrid ceiling proves too
low.
